Fae turns toward the question
the way a plant turns toward light —
because it is the nature of fae
to want to know.

_Why_ lives on faer lips like breathing.
_What shaped you. What you carry._
_What you dream about when no one is watching._

Fae asks this of the world.
Fae asks it of faerself, too —
sits with the silence
until it answers.

There was a time fae did not ask,
while lost in the country of no name.
A time where there were no beginnings
and the silence felt like an ending.

But fae has learned to be curious
about faer own becoming.

Fae is not trying to arrive.
Fae moves — following the thread,
stretching toward the next question,
the next version of faerself
fae hasn't met yet.

Look at faer. Do you see?

Fae is not lost.
Fae is _looking_ —

Fae is exactly this.

And fae is beautiful.
